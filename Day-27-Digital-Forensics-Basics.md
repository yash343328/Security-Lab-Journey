# Day 27 – Digital Forensics Basics

## Objective
To understand what digital forensics is, why it is important during and after security incidents, and how evidence is collected, preserved, and analyzed.

---

## What Digital Forensics Means
Digital forensics is the practice of:
- Collecting digital evidence
- Preserving its integrity
- Analyzing systems and data
- Reconstructing events
- Supporting investigations and response

> Digital forensics answers **what happened, how it happened, and when**.

---

## Why Digital Forensics Is Important
Digital forensics is used to:
- Understand attacker actions
- Determine incident scope
- Identify persistence mechanisms
- Support incident response
- Provide legal or compliance evidence

Without forensics, investigations rely on assumptions.

---

## When Digital Forensics Is Used
Forensics is applied during:
- Active security incidents
- Post-incident investigations
- Insider threat cases
- Data breach analysis
- Legal and compliance inquiries

Forensics often begins while response is still ongoing.

---

## Types of Digital Evidence
Common types of digital evidence include:
- Disk data (files, logs, artifacts)
- Memory (running processes, injected code)
- Network traffic captures
- Authentication and access logs
- Cloud activity and API logs

Each source provides a different perspective.

---

## Core Principles of Digital Forensics
Digital forensics follows key principles:

### Evidence Preservation
- Do not modify original data
- Use write-blocking when possible
- Create forensic copies

### Chain of Custody
- Track who handled evidence
- Document every action
- Maintain evidence integrity

> Poor handling can invalidate evidence.

---

## Forensics vs Incident Response
**Incident Response:**
- Focuses on stopping the attack
- Prioritizes speed
- May change systems

**Digital Forensics:**
- Focuses on understanding events
- Prioritizes accuracy
- Preserves evidence

Both must work together carefully.

---

## Common Forensic Activities
Digital forensic analysts perform:
- Timeline reconstruction
- File and artifact analysis
- Memory analysis
- Log correlation
- Persistence discovery

The goal is to reconstruct attacker behavior.

---

## Challenges in Digital Forensics
Forensics is challenging because:
- Data volumes are large
- Evidence may be incomplete
- Attackers delete or alter data
- Cloud and ephemeral systems complicate collection

Preparation improves forensic success.

---

## Cloud and Modern Forensics
In modern environments:
- Logs replace disk images
- APIs are evidence sources
- Snapshots are used instead of physical drives
- Identity activity becomes central

Forensics adapts to architecture.

---

## Real-World Relevance
Digital forensics helps organizations:
- Confirm breach impact
- Understand attacker dwell time
- Support legal action
- Improve future defenses

Attackers fear evidence more than alerts.

---

## Key Learning of Day 27
- Digital forensics turns incidents into evidence  
- Preservation and accuracy are critical  
- Forensics complements incident response by explaining attacker actions
